GOLF

i dont actually like golf that much but ive done it my entire life and my body can never truly forget how to take this large metal club and swing it at up to 90mph to hit this tiny ball and send it flying through the air until it travels up to 210 yards away from me in the direction of a distant and inscrutable goal (specifically a flag that is colored blue, which is blending into the sky behind it and now i cannot perceive it with my POOR VISION)

but while im talking about it these are my TOP FIVE FAVORITE CLUBS. links have NOTHING to do with my actual choice of club i dont remember what brand i use. ask again later.

  1. DRIVER

    this thing NEVER LETS ME DOWN. i believe that there is a psychological element to it in that because golf is a game of confidence, your game naturally improves when you are confident that you are going to do well. because i am so confident in my ability to play using my driver, i do not hesitate on my swings, i do not overthink it, i do not allow my mentality to seep into my game and sabotage me. in this way i only ever hit well with my driver. i am also generally just good with my driver as a fact, but i do not have such unshakable confidence in any other club. you may think that my awareness of my skill in my driver being largely linked to my belief that it is a club that will never serve me wrong would inherently undermine the idea that it could never let me down if i know that it is based upon something as flimsy as my own thoughts, which can change at any moment, but you would be wrong and a fool, because my confidence is also rooted in my genuine skill, and i am not so weak as to let the knowledge that my confidence is a construct of my own imagination stop it from fueling me.

  2. SEVEN IRON

    irons are always a very pleasant kind of club to play with, but the seven iron is just that happy middle ground between all of my irons. it is difficult to describe what, exactly, the difference is between a nine iron and a five iron, other than the distance of course, but the lower the number the more strict the swing is. however a nine iron does not go far at all (mine averages around 115 yards), while my seven goes about 135. the five iron is quite satisfying when i hit it well, but there is an inverse relationship between my reliability and skill and the distance i can hit with the club. with the exception of the driver. my driver is fantastic.

  3. SAND WEDGE

    a similarly pleasing club. it is the one that goes shortest, and is very easy to swing generally, but because it is such a short club you must use it in your short game. it must be used when you are going to chip on the green, or in the bunkers, or when you must lay up, or get out of the woods. it is so pleasant and kind to me but because i must use this club in only the most challenging of situations (i am not good at my short game) this one falls down the rankings because the environment you use it in is difficult by no fault of its own. with the driver i can choose where i go from. i am always teed up to the perfect position. this is not the same. with the sand wedge only god can decide what your circumstances are. it is stressful.

  4. PITCHING WEDGE

    pitching wedge. o pitching wedge. u serve me so well when i have to hit across the water youre so cool. youre my beloved for rivers and par 3 holes. you have never let me down but the cases in which i must use you are so rare. come home. i miss you
